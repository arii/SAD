Mind Hand Heart Innovation Grant

This project is funded by the Mind Hand Heart Innovation Grant. The goal of my project is to place public artificial lightbox locations on campus for people with Seasonal Affective Disorder (SAD).

Where to find lightboxes

Currently, lightboxes are set up in the telephone booths of the MIT Stata Center.

Innovation Fund

The MindHandHeart Innovation Fund seeks to leverage the enthusiasm and problem-solving skills of the MIT community to find new and inventive ways of increasing awareness about mental health, building communities of support, and promoting life and wellness skills.

Seasonal Affective Disorder

The following is a summary from the SAD wikipedia page.

Seasonal affective disorder (SAD), also known as winter depression, winter blues, summer depression, summertime sadness, or seasonal depression, is a mood disorder subset in which people who have normal mental health throughout most of the year experience depressive symptoms at the same time each year, most commonly in the winter. Treatments for classic (winter-based) seasonal affective disorder include light therapy, medication, ionized-air administration, cognitive-behavioral therapy and carefully timed supplementation of the hormone melatonin.
